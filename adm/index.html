Alternative Delivery Mode

Welcome to the Alternative Delivery Mode (ADM) — a flexible and inclusive learning approach designed to ensure that every learner has access to quality education, regardless of circumstances.

Explore Now

Purpose

Empowering Learners Beyond the Classroom.

Provide access to basic education when learners cannot attend school physically due to various barriers (e.g., congestion, geographic isolation, socio-economic factors). Rooted in the Philippine Constitution's mandate to make education accessible to all as well as the goals of lifelong learning, and independent study modalities.

  • ADM Commitment to Learners

    To provide inclusive and flexible learning opportunities that ensure quality education for every learner, regardless of circumstances.

Homeschooling

Homeschooling as an ADM

DepEd Memo 10, s.2020 on Addendum to DepEd Order No. 21, s.2019 – Recognized ADM, with guidelines for implementation.

Homeschooling

is an alternative delivery mode where parents/guardians take full responsibility for basic education curriculum, instruction, materials, and academic monitoring. Homeschooling classes can be held at home, learning center, or any location suitable for instruction.

Learning and Service Continuity

School Learning and Service Continuity Plans should be implemented and updated based on context
(e.g., modular distance learning, online classes, blended learning).

Must identify appropriate ADMs that are:

Context-based and responsive to learner needs

Activated during calamities or emergencies

Ensure continuity of education

Operational Guidelines for Calamities or Emergencies

Prepared. Responsive. Accountable

School Head ensures the following

  • Shift to safer, high heat index, earthquakes or health emergencies (e.g., pandemic) modes
  • May cancel in-person classes when deemed necessary
  • Alternative locations outside of School Year Clouds allowed as needed
  • Prioritize student and teacher safety
